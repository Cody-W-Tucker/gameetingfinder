GAMeetingFinder QA Brief

This brief summarizes the most important public-site issues found during a browser-based review. The goal is to highlight what users experience, why it matters, and what should be fixed first.

Site Reviewed
gameetingfinder.com
Review Date
May 17, 2026
Total Findings
6 issues
Severity Mix
6 Medium

Executive Summary

Primary Theme

Several high-intent actions look available but do not take users where they expect to go.

Highest Risk Area

Recovery and meeting-discovery flows contain misrouted links and interrupted user journeys.

Client Impact

Users may lose trust, abandon core tasks, or miss the fastest path to active meetings and help resources.

Findings

1. Search button appears broken

Medium

The homepage header includes a visible Search meetings button, but clicking it does not navigate, open a search panel, or provide any visible response.

  • What the user sees: a button that looks active but does nothing.
  • Why it matters: this reduces trust right at the top of the homepage.
  • Recommended fix: connect the button to the meeting search experience or remove it until it is ready.
Observed result: clicking the search button leaves the user on the same homepage with no response.

2. Recovery links send users to the homepage

Medium

On the Resources and Research pages, links that suggest they will take users to meetings instead route back to the homepage.

  • What the user sees: “find a virtual GA meeting” and “Find Meetings” links that do not go to the meeting directory.
  • Why it matters: users in urgent recovery flows are delayed or redirected into marketing content.
  • Recommended fix: route all meeting-intent links to /find-a-meeting.
Observed result: multiple meeting-oriented links on the Research page point back to the homepage.

3. Meetings landing page CTA loops back to home

Medium

The featured call-to-action on /meetings says Find a Meeting Happening Right Now, but the link target is the homepage.

  • What the user sees: a strong CTA that does not continue the meeting flow.
  • Why it matters: this creates a dead-end on a core discovery page.
  • Recommended fix: link directly to a current-meetings view or active meeting filter.
Observed result: the “Join a Meeting Now” CTA on the meetings landing page routes back to home.

4. Newsletter modal interrupts meeting browsing

Medium

While browsing the live meeting directory, a newsletter signup modal appears automatically after a short delay.

  • What the user sees: a marketing popup over the meeting list.
  • Why it matters: it interrupts the highest-intent recovery flow on the site.
  • Recommended fix: suppress the modal on meeting-critical pages or delay it until after meaningful engagement.
Observed result: the newsletter modal appears automatically over the meeting directory.

5. Share action appears inert on meeting cards

Medium

Meeting cards expose a Share this meeting action, but clicking it does not show a share dialog, copy confirmation, or any visible feedback.

  • What the user sees: a share control that appears non-responsive.
  • Why it matters: users cannot confidently pass meetings to sponsors, family, or peers.
  • Recommended fix: trigger a native share flow, copy-to-clipboard confirmation, or a visible share modal.
Observed result: clicking “Share this meeting” produced no visible share state or confirmation.

6. Partnership form signals required fields but does not enforce them

Medium

The partnership inquiry modal labels Full Name * and Email Address * as required, but they are not enforced as required fields.

  • What the user sees: a form that visually marks required fields without matching behavior.
  • Why it matters: this creates confusion and weakens confidence in the form.
  • Recommended fix: align the labels and actual validation rules so the form behaves consistently.
Observed result: the modal shows required asterisks, but the required-field enforcement does not match.
